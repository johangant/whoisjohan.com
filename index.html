About me

Practical solutions for teams that need things to work

My career started in top-tier higher education and has taken me through leading digital agencies, charity, policy making, media and professional services teams with my present focus on UK public sector and government departments.

I have worked as a developer, architect, project lead and team lead. That mix has taught me where projects usually get stuck: unclear ownership, fragile environments, slow releases and decisions that fall apart once the work starts.

My focus now is platform engineering, team dynamics and pragmatic AI literacy: better environments, safer releases, sensible hosting, useful automation and the habits that help teams spend more time on the things that actually matter.

Where I can help

Platform engineering

I come from a web development background, with a practical experience across PHP, Drupal, WordPress, Python and C#/Java if I go back far enough. I use tools like Docker, cloud platforms like AWS/GCP/Azure and modern CI/CD services.

I am strongest where code, hosting, environments and team workflow meet. I help make those parts simpler, more reliable and easier for teams to own.

Pragmatic AI supported delivery

AI tools are here and are rapidly establishing themselves in workplace teams

I help teams use AI tools as part of a disciplined software development lifecycle, from discovery and prototyping through review, testing, documentation and maintenance.

That means knowing where tools are useful, where they are risky, and how to keep human judgement, security, maintainability and delivery ownership in the loop.

Technical leadership

I have recruited, built and managed developer teams, both remote and on-site. I have also delivered pitches, interviews, planning and established delivery standards.

I work well between senior people, product teams and engineers. I can turn technical concerns into clear choices, without losing the detail that matters.

Technical direction and mentoring

I help teams make technical decisions they can live with: reviewing builds, choosing hosting routes, spotting delivery risks, sense checking supplier proposals and improving engineering habits through real work.

I also help engineers build clearer judgement around AI assisted development, so any gains do not come at the cost of quality, context or accountability.
