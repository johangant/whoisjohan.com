About me

Practical engineering for teams that need things to work.

I have spent most of my career in UK digital agencies, working with public sector, charity, media, education, policy and professional services teams.

I have worked as a developer, architect, project lead and team lead. That mix has taught me where projects usually get stuck: unclear ownership, fragile environments, slow releases and decisions that fall apart once the work starts.

My focus now is platform engineering: better environments, safer releases, sensible hosting, useful automation and the habits that help teams keep moving after launch.

What I do

Where I can help.

Platform engineering

I come from a web development background, with practical experience across Drupal, GatsbyJS/React, Python, WordPress and Node.js. I use Docker, DDEV and cloud tooling regularly.

I am strongest where code, hosting, environments and team workflow meet. I help make those parts simpler, more reliable and easier for teams to own.

Technical direction

I help teams make technical decisions they can live with. That can mean reviewing a build, choosing a hosting route, spotting delivery risks, or sense-checking a supplier proposal.

My advice stays close to the work: what the team can run, what the system needs, and what will still make sense after launch.

Technical leadership

I have recruited, built and managed developer teams, both remote and on-site. I have also led pitches, interviews, planning and delivery standards.

I work well between senior people, product teams and engineers. I can turn technical concerns into clear choices, without losing the detail that matters.

Mentoring

I help engineers grow through real work: clearer judgement, better habits, stronger delivery and a more honest view of what they want from their career.
